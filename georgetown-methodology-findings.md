# Georgetown University CHIR Methodology for 586,581 Case Analysis

## Research Publication Details
- **Title:** "Independent Dispute Resolution Process 2024 Data: High Volume, More Provider Wins"
- **Institution:** Georgetown University Center on Health Insurance Reforms (CHIR)
- **Authors:** Jack Hoadley, Kennah Watts, and Zachary Baron
- **Publication Date:** July 1, 2025
- **Data Period:** Q1-Q2 2024

## Data Sources Identified

### Primary Data Source: Federal IDR Public Use Files (PUFs)
- **Source:** Federal agencies released public use files (PUFs) with data on resolved IDR cases for 2023
- **2024 Data:** Agencies released PUFs for the first two quarters of 2024 in March 2025
- **Content:** Files include information on provider and payer, as well as offer amounts from each party
- **Format:** Data expressed as percentage of qualifying payment amount (QPA)
- **Additional Information:** Files include information on prevailing offer as determined by IDR entity

### Secondary Data Sources
- **Federal IDR Supplemental Tables:** Used for 2023 and 2024 analysis
- **Agency Supplemental Tables:** Referenced for additional context
- **Stakeholder Discussions:** Georgetown conducted discussions with industry stakeholders

## Case Volume Analysis
- **Q1-Q2 2024:** 586,581 filed IDR cases
- **Comparison:** Nearly as high as all of 2023 (657,040 filed cases)
- **Payment Determinations:** About 335,000 payment determinations in Q1-Q2 2024 vs. 200,000 in all of 2023

## Analytical Approach Identified

### Trend Analysis Framework
Georgetown's analysis examined trends across multiple dimensions:
1. **Provider and payer types**
2. **Offer amounts** (expressed as percentage of QPA)
3. **Geographic locations**
4. **IDR entities performance**
5. **Case eligibility and challenges**

### Comparative Analysis
- **Temporal Comparison:** 2023 vs. Q1-Q2 2024 data
- **Cross-sectional Analysis:** Across different provider types, specialties, and geographic regions
- **Outcome Analysis:** Provider vs. payer win rates and payment amounts

### Data Processing Methods
- **Percentage Calculations:** All offer amounts expressed as percentage of QPA
- **Median Analysis:** Focus on median prevailing offers rather than means
- **Rate Calculations:** Challenge rates, eligibility rates, win rates
- **Volume Tracking:** Case filing volumes and processing speeds

## Key Methodological Elements

### Data Quality Considerations
- **Portal Suspensions:** Noted that 2023 data may be affected by periods when IDR portal was suspended
- **Eligibility Challenges:** 45% of cases challenged as ineligible in 2024 vs. 37% in 2023
- **One-Party Submissions:** About one in six determinations made with only one party submitting offers

### Analytical Limitations Acknowledged
- **Speculation on Decision Rationale:** "Without public reporting of the rationale for IDR entity decisions, observers can only speculate"
- **Stakeholder Perspectives:** Balanced presentation of provider vs. payer viewpoints
- **Data Completeness:** Acknowledgment of potential data gaps due to system suspensions
