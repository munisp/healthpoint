## Georgetown Licensing Requirements Analysis

### **Public Domain vs. Proprietary Elements**

#### **✅ Public Domain (No License Required):**
- **Published Research Findings:** Georgetown's published papers and reports are public domain
- **CMS PUF Data:** Federal data is publicly available and free to use
- **Statistical Methodologies:** Standard statistical methods (regression, correlation) are not proprietary
- **General Insights:** Published findings about win rates, QPA multipliers, etc. are public knowledge

#### **⚠️ Potential Licensing Requirements:**
- **Proprietary Datasets:** Any non-public Georgetown datasets or enhanced data
- **Specific Algorithms:** Proprietary analytical methods developed by Georgetown researchers
- **Software Tools:** Any Georgetown-developed software or analytical platforms
- **Trademark Usage:** Using "Georgetown" branding or institutional endorsement

#### **🎯 Recommended Approach:**
**Use Georgetown's published research as inspiration and validation, but develop independent proprietary methodologies that don't require licensing.**

### **Legal Risk Assessment: LOW**
Georgetown's published research is academic work in the public domain. Using their findings as a foundation for independent development carries minimal legal risk, similar to how financial firms use academic research to develop trading algorithms.
